Driver's Health & Safety Manual

🤖 AI & Research Mode Active: This version is synced with the SALA ML Engine and logging biomechanical data for the PhD research of Dr. Natalia Bellan.

The SALA (Safe-Load Analytics) system is your personal biomechanical assistant. Our goal is simple: to ensure you return home as healthy as you started your shift. By monitoring real-time physical strain, we prevent long-term injuries and medical leave.

Core Philosophy: We don't just track cargo; we track the impact of the cargo on your body through Machine Learning.

1. The Risk Index (Ri) & ML Engine

Your app displays a percentage calculated in real-time. Unlike static charts, the SALA AI evaluates:

  • Cumulative Fatigue: Long driving hours (initialized at 2.0h) reduce muscle core stability.
  • Dynamic Thermal Sync: The system automatically fetches local weather to adjust tendon elasticity margins.
  • BPM Rule: Your weight vs. cargo weight proportionality.

2. Real-Time Alert Protocols

⚠️ YELLOW ALERT (50-79%): Fatigue detected. Adjust your lifting posture. Use mechanical aids for the next 30 minutes.
🚫 RED ALERT (80%+): Critical musculoskeletal saturation. Stop manual handling immediately. Take a 5-minute active recovery break.

3. The Smart Scanner System

Using the [ ] SCAN PACKAGE button updates your load profile instantly:

  • Automatic Weight Logging: Each scan increments your cargo weight by a safety-standard factor (0.01t).
  • Hybrid Input: You can manually adjust "Heavy Packages" (>25kg) for more accurate risk profiling.

Scientific Note: Continuous logging helps the SALA Engine learn your specific fatigue threshold over time.

4. Environmental & Research Factors

When the "Detection Temp" syncs, the system adjusts for Vasoconstriction (reduced blood flow in cold weather). As part of our ongoing research, anonymized biomechanical data is logged to improve safety standards for all Nordic drivers.

5. Data Privacy

SALA is a Preventative Health Tool. Your data is encrypted and used to justify the need for better ergonomic equipment and safer shift patterns.
