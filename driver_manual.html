Driver's Health & Safety Manual

The SALA (Safe-Load Analytics) system is your personal biomechanical assistant. Our goal is simple: to ensure you return home as healthy as you started your shift. By monitoring real-time physical strain, we prevent long-term injuries and medical leave (sykemelding).

Core Philosophy: We don't just track cargo; we track the impact of the cargo on your body.

1. The Risk Index (Ri) Explained

Your app displays a percentage representing your current musculoskeletal load. This isn't just a number—it’s a scientific calculation based on:

  • Cumulative Fatigue: Long driving hours reduce muscle core stability.
  • Inertial Impact: Sudden movements with heavy packages increase lumbar torque.
  • Thermal Stress: Working in Norwegian winter temperatures makes tendons more brittle.

2. Real-Time Alert Protocols

When the system detects a high-risk movement or an unsafe accumulation of strain, it will trigger alerts:

⚠️ YELLOW ALERT (50-79%): Fatigue detected. Adjust your lifting posture. Use mechanical aids (pallets/trolleys) for the next 30 minutes.
🚫 RED ALERT (80%+): Critical musculoskeletal saturation. Stop manual handling immediately. Take a 5-minute active recovery break.

3. The "Package Weight" Safety Rule

SALA applies the Body Mass Percentage Rule. Every time you scan a package, the system evaluates the effort required:

  • Under 25kg: Standard handling. Ensure center of gravity is close to your body.
  • Over 25kg: High-risk category. The system logs these as "Heavy Impacts". Continuous lifting of these without mechanical support will rapidly spike your Risk Index.

Scientific Note: Lifting 25kg with an extended back can generate over 300kg of pressure on the L5-S1 spinal discs.

4. Winter Operations (Larvik & Nordic Region)

Cold weather is a silent risk factor. When the app detects temperatures below 0°C:

  • The system increases the "Safety Margin" (Sf).
  • Muscles require more oxygen but receive less due to vasoconstriction.
  • Action: Allow the "Detection Temp" on your app to sync. The system will automatically suggest more frequent, shorter breaks.

5. Data Privacy & Your Rights

SALA is a Preventative Health Tool, not a surveillance device. Your biomechanical data is used to justify investments in better equipment and to prevent workplace accidents. Your employer sees the safety of the fleet, while you control your individual health status.
