SALA: Safe-Load Analytics

National Strategic Consolidation Summary

Technical Status: The SALA engine is technically dependent on real-time data streaming from Statens vegvesen to accurately calibrate the Surface Factor ($S_f$) and topographical spinal load across Norway's diverse road network.

1. National Project Vision

SALA is the nationwide standard for Biomechanical Governance. It leverages Norway's public road infrastructure data to protect transport workers from spinal injuries (L5/S1) across all domestic corridors, from the E18 in the south to the Arctic routes in the north.

Biomechanic Infrastructure

  • Topographical Adjustment: Risk Index ($R_i$) is dynamically scaled based on road inclination data.
  • Public Data Integration: Direct ingestion of road friction and weather data from National Road Banks.
  • Δf Scaling: Fatigue monitoring adjusted for extreme Nordic environments.

National Compliance

  • AML § 4-4: Standardized automation of the Working Environment Act for all Norwegian logistics hubs.
  • Arbeidstilsynet: Real-time auditing capabilities for the Labour Inspection Authority.
  • National Safety Delegates: Cross-regional access for Verneombud.

2. National Operating Modules

  • Central Risk Dashboard: Fleet overview categorized by regions (South, West, Central, North).
  • Mountain Pass Protocol: Specific biomechanical triggers for high-inclination routes (e.g., RV7, E134).
  • Arctic Operating Mode: Maximum sensitivity for vibration (WBV) and friction in polar latitudes.
  • Mechanical Mandate: Uniform enforcement of mechanical aid requirements across the entire road network.
