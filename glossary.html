SALA • MASTER PROJECT GLOSSARY (v1.0)

Project: Safe-Load Analytics (Norwegian Logistics Corridor)
Classification: Internal / Regulatory Compliance (HSE)

1.0 CORE BIOMECHANICAL METRICS (SALA ENGINE)

1.1 Ri (Risk Index) The system's core algorithm. A dynamic metric (0-100%) that processes real-time telemetry to predict the probability of acute or cumulative musculoskeletal injury.
1.2 L5/S1 Compression Force A specific measure of torque and pressure exerted on the intervertebral disc between the fifth lumbar vertebra and the sacrum. The SALA system triggers automatic alerts when this force exceeds biomechanical safety limits during lifting tasks.
1.3 Δf (Fatigue Coefficient / Utmattelseskoeffisient) A mathematical factor that degrades the driver's resilience based on shift duration and effort history over the previous 24 hours.
1.4 WBV (Whole-Body Vibration) Exposure of the whole body to mechanical vibrations through the vehicle seat, monitored according to ISO 2631-1 standards to prevent nerve fatigue and chronic lower back pain (lumbago).

2.0 OPERATIONAL & ENVIRONMENTAL PARAMETERS

2.1 Sf (Surface Factor / Underlagsfaktor) A risk multiplier applied to $R_i$ when the sensor detects surface instability (e.g., uneven terrain or slippery conditions).
2.2 CoF (Coefficient of Friction) A measure of surface stability. Under the "Arctic Protocol," surfaces with a $CoF < 0.1$ (glare ice) automatically activate maximum safety mode.
2.3 Arctic Protocol A system configuration applied in high-latitude regions (Northern Norway), where fatigue limits are adjusted for temperatures below -15°C.
2.4 Larvik Hub Sector The project’s pilot operational district, focused on risk management between the port terminal and the E18 logistics corridor.

3.0 LEGAL & REGULATORY FRAMEWORK (NORWAY)

3.1 AML (Arbeidsmiljøloven) The Norwegian Working Environment Act. The SALA system is specifically designed to automate compliance with Section 4-4, which requires work to be adapted to the employee’s physical capabilities.
3.2 Verneombud (Safety Delegate) The workers' elected representative who has legal authority to review SALA risk logs and, if necessary, halt unsafe operations.
3.3 YSK (Yrkessjåførkompetanse) The mandatory professional driver qualification. SALA monitors the validity of this certification integrated with driver risk data.
3.4 37.5h Standard Week The Norwegian legal limit for a standard work week, used as the baseline for calculating homeostatic muscle recovery.
3.5 Arbeidstilsynet The Norwegian Labour Inspection Authority. SALA generates automated reports following this body's standards in case of audits or incidents.

4.0 SYSTEM PROTOCOLS

4.1 Mechanical Mandate An intervention protocol that prohibits manual cargo handling and mandates the exclusive use of mechanical aids when $R_i$ crosses the defined threshold (usually 85%).
4.2 Recovery Interval A mandatory 11-hour rest period between shifts, validated by the system to reset the accumulated fatigue coefficient ($\Delta f$).
4.3 Real-time Telemetry The constant stream of data from wearable sensors and the vehicle to the Management Dashboard.
